Gibson Assembly is a high-efficiency method of assembling multiple DNA fargements in a single reaction step [Gibson et al. 2009].

PCR is used to generate DNA fragments with overlapping end sequences (~40bp). After purification (by gel-extraction), these DNA fragments are joined in a single reaction vessel through the activity of three enzymes: T5 exonuclease, DNA polymerase, Taq DNA ligase.

The assembly reaction proceed as follows:

  1. T5 exonuclease excises nucleotides from the 5’ end of dsDNA molecules, exposing 3’ overhangs.
Benefits
  • Any DNA sequences amenable to PCR amplification can be joined, irrespective of the presence of restriction sites.
  • Complete control of goal sequence (scarless).
  • Up to 20 DNA sequences can be assembled in a single 1 hour reaction.
Disadvantages
  • Cost and delivery time of custom primers.
  • DNA sequences with high Tm secondary structures or homologous sequences at termini assemble with low efficiency and can result rearrangements.
